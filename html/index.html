Knowledge Page

What is Nifty?

Learn about India's most popular stock market index.

Read more

Nifty 50 is a benchmark stock market index that represents the performance of the top 50 large companies listed on the National Stock Exchange (NSE) of India. It helps investors understand the overall market trend and economic performance.
What is Sensex?

Understand the stock market index of the Bombay Stock Exchange.

Read more

Sensex is the benchmark index of the Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE). It consists of 30 well-established companies from different sectors and is used to measure the overall performance of the Indian stock market.
Intraday vs Delivery Trading

Learn the difference between two common trading methods.

Read more

Intraday trading involves buying and selling shares on the same day, while delivery trading allows investors to hold shares for days, months, or even years. Intraday trading is generally riskier, whereas delivery trading is more suitable for long-term investors.
What is a Dividend?

Discover how companies reward their shareholders.

Read more

A dividend is a portion of a company's profits distributed to its shareholders. Companies may pay dividends regularly as a reward to investors for holding their shares.
What is a Demat Account?

Understand the account required for investing in stocks.

Read more

A Demat (Dematerialized) Account is used to hold shares and securities in electronic form. It eliminates the need for physical share certificates and is essential for buying and selling stocks in India.
What is an IPO?

Learn how companies enter the stock market.

Read more

An Initial Public Offering (IPO) is the process through which a private company offers its shares to the public for the first time. It allows companies to raise capital and gives investors an opportunity to become shareholders.
